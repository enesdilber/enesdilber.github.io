2. One-Way MANOVA¶

Classical ANOVA: $$ Y_{ij} \\ i^{th} treatment, j^{th} observation $$

MANOVA: $$ Y_{ijk} \\ i^{th} treatment, j^{th} observation, k^{th} variable $$

Therefore only difference is, now we have more than one variable corresponds to $i^{th}$ treatment and $j^{th}$ observation.

Before carrying out a MANOVA, first check the model assumptions:¶

1 The data from group i has common mean vector $\mu_i$¶

2 The data from all groups have common variance-covariance matrix $\Sigma$¶

3 Independence: The subjects are independently sampled¶

4 Normality: The data are multivariate normally distributed¶
